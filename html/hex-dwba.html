Hex-dwba
This program computes the T-matrix from the first or second order of the distorted wave Born approximation,
\[ T_{\mathrm{DWBA2}} = T_{\mathrm{DWBA1}} + DD + EE + (-1)^S (DE + ED) \ . \]
In the distorted wave approach the waves are no longer planar as in the notirious PWBA; they are influenced by the potential. This is represented by replacement of plane waves by the solutions of the distorted wave equation
\[ \left( -\frac{1}{2}\frac{\mathrm{d}^2}{\mathrm{d}r^2} + \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{2r^2} + U(r) \right) \chi_\ell(k,r) = \frac{k^2}{2} \chi_\ell(k,r) \ , \]
where \(U(r)\) is the so-called distorting potential. The second-order contribution to the T-matrix comes from four indistinguishable processes which do or do not include exchange of particles before (\(*E\)) or after (\(E*\)) the excitation to the intermediate state.
